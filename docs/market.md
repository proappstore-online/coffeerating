# Market Research & Competitive Analysis

## Market Size

- Global specialty coffee market: **~$111.5B (2025)**, growing at **10.8% CAGR** → projected $251.7B by 2033 (Grand View Research).
- **66% of American adults** drink coffee daily (NCA 2025).
- **18–24 age cohort** leads specialty coffee spending at **32.3% revenue share** — CoffeeRating's primary demographic.
- **Away-from-home consumption = 72.4% of specialty coffee revenue** — exactly the use-case CoffeeRating targets (rating a coffee while at the café).

---

## Competitor Analysis

### Beanhunter
- Café discovery and review platform. User accounts, public profiles, written reviews.
- Requires social identity. Review-focused (text), not photo-first.
- Gap: no anonymous quick-submit, no photo-as-primary-signal.

### Kava
- Coffee journal / personal log app. Private by default.
- Gap: private (not crowd-sourced), no public map.

### Tasting Grounds
- Curated café listings. Editor/admin-verified content.
- Gap: not user-generated ratings, requires account, no photo-first flow.

### Coffunity
- Social coffee community. Followers, friend feeds, public profiles.
- Gap: heavy social graph — the opposite of frictionless anonymity.

### Good Fika
- Café recommendation app. Editor-curated, not crowd-sourced.
- Gap: no user ratings, no photo uploads.

### Specialty Coffee Association resources
- Educational, not consumer-facing rating apps.

---

## The Gap CoffeeRating Fills

Every major competitor requires **social identity** — public profiles, followers, friend feeds, or curator-verified shop listings. None offer:

1. **Truly anonymous ratings** — no public identity attached to a rating.
2. **Photo-first submission** — photo is required and the visual hero.
3. **Frictionless speed** — target < 30 seconds from open to submitted.
4. **Exact drink logging** — free-text drink description (bean brand, milk brand, temp, etc.).

CoffeeRating's positioning: **"Rate this exact cup, right now, no one needs to know it was you."**

## Go-To-Market Notes

- Primary acquisition: word-of-mouth at cafés (someone sees another person rating a coffee).
- Secondary: specialty coffee communities (Reddit r/Coffee, Instagram coffee hashtags).
- No social graph = no virality loop, but also no churn from social anxiety.
- Value prop for café owners: free crowd-sourced quality signal (could be a future B2B angle).
